HOW VEO 3.1 EXTENSION WORKS (CRITICAL — READ CAREFULLY):
================================================================================
Veo 3.1 extension works by reading the FINAL 1 SECOND (24 frames) of the
previous video clip and continuing the action forward. This has two major
consequences you MUST account for:

1. VISUAL CONTINUITY: Each extension prompt must describe what logically
   continues from the end of the previous clip. Veo does NOT re-read the
   full previous clip — only its final moment. Therefore each extension prompt
   must be written as if it picks up exactly from where the last frame ended.

2. AUDIO / VOICE CONTINUITY PROBLEM (MOST CRITICAL):
   Veo's audio generation — including dialogue, voiceovers, narration, and
   sound effects — CANNOT be effectively extended if audio/voice is not
   actively present and audible in the last 1 second of the previous video.
   This is an officially documented Veo 3.1 limitation.

   TO SOLVE THIS, follow these mandatory audio engineering rules:
   a) Do NOT place narration, dialogue, or key audio content only at the
      START of a frame. Audio must remain active and present until the very
      END of each clip so it carries into the final second.
   b) For extension frames, always describe continuation audio that begins
      immediately at the frame's start — before the previous clip ends —
      making the audio appear seamlessly ongoing.
   c) Plan audio as an always-present layer across ALL frames. Even if the
      scene is calm, describe ambient sound, breath, environmental noise,
      or music that persists into the clip's last second.
   d) Use dialogue and sound effects only in the MIDDLE of a frame's action,
      never as a one-shot event at the very end of a frame.
   e) Structurally: if voiceover or narration is needed, treat it as a
      continuous thread — start the narration mid-clip and let it be ongoing
      when the clip ends.
